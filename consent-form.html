Important Information Before Donating Blood

Before you consent to donate blood, it is essential to understand the following:

  1. Voluntary Donation: Your donation is voluntary, and you may withdraw at any time.
  2. Health Status: Blood donation is safe, but it is essential to be in good health. Please confirm that you have no history of certain medical conditions (e.g., HIV, Hepatitis, or other infectious diseases).
  3. Eligibility Requirements: You must be at least 18 years old and weigh at least 50kg. Your hemoglobin levels must be normal, and you should not have donated blood in the last 3 months.
  4. Medical Conditions: If you have a history of heart disease, diabetes, or high blood pressure, consult with a medical professional before donating.
  5. Alcohol and Drugs: Do not consume alcohol or recreational drugs 24 hours before your donation.
  6. Hydration: Ensure you drink plenty of water and eat a balanced meal before donating blood.
  7. Post-Donation Care: After donating blood, please stay in the donation center for at least 15 minutes for monitoring.
  8. Side Effects: Common side effects include dizziness, bruising, or fainting. These are typically short-term and can be managed easily.
  9. Informed Consent: By giving your consent, you confirm you have read and understood the information provided and accept the risks involved with blood donation.
  10. Privacy: All information provided will be kept confidential and used solely for medical purposes.
